Alan Ramponi

Welcome! I am a senior researcher at Fondazione Bruno Kessler where I am part of the DH group. My research focuses on natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning (ML) for studying, analyzing, and generating human languages in their diverse social/linguistic/cultural contexts. Broadly, I am interested in:

  • Multilingual NLP: handling multiple languages, scripts, and cultural nuances
  • Variability in language (dia/sociolects) and annotation (human subjectivity)
  • Generalizability: dealing w/ data scarcity and domain shifts (transfer learning)
  • Societal impact: ethics, bias, and fairness in NLP/ML (data, labels, and models)

My research is informed by linguistics and social sciences and work is cross-cutting (e.g., mis/disinformation countering, argument mining, computational dialectology).
